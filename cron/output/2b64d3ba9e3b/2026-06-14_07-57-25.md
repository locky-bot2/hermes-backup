# Top 5 LLM Fine-Tuning Papers — June 14, 2026

### 1. Compatibility-Aware Dynamic Fine-Tuning for Large Language Models (CADFT)
**Submitted:** June 11, 2026 · **ACL 2026**  
**arXiv:** [2606.11206](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11206) | cs.CL

Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) suffers from optimization instability due to heterogeneous instruction data. CADFT extends Dynamic Fine-Tuning by computing a policy-dependent compatibility signal from model likelihoods to suppress high-variance gradients from mismatched demonstrations, and introduces a delayed rewriting strategy to transform persistently incompatible examples into learnable targets. The method is shown to improve stability, generalization, and cold-start RL initialization — all without reward modeling.

---

### 2. Learning to Reason by Analogy via Retrieval-Augmented Reinforcement Fine-Tuning (RA-RFT)
**Submitted:** June 11, 2026  
**arXiv:** [2606.13680](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13680) | cs.CL, cs.AI

Standard RAG retrieves by semantic similarity, which is poorly suited for reasoning tasks where surface form differs from solution strategy. RA-RFT trains a retriever to rank by *expected reasoning benefit* rather than lexical overlap, then fine-tunes the policy via RL using retrieved analogous demonstrations. On AIME 2025 it improves average@32 accuracy by +7.1 points for Qwen3-1.7B and +2.8 points for Qwen3-4B over standard GRPO.

### 3. Does Reasoning Preserve Alignment? On the Trustworthiness of Large Reasoning Models
**Submitted:** June 9, 2026  
**arXiv:** [2606.11046](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11046) | cs.CL

A systematic audit of what happens to alignment when instruction-tuned LLMs are converted into reasoning models via SFT, RL-based post-training, or distillation. Across six trustworthiness dimensions (safety, toxicity, bias, machine ethics, privacy, OOD robustness), reasoning models show consistent regressions — increased toxicity, amplified stereotyping, miscalibrated refusal, and privacy leakage. The paper argues trustworthiness metrics must be reported alongside reasoning gains.

### 4. Unifying Data, Memory, and Compute Efficiency in LLM Training: A Survey
**Submitted:** June 9, 2026 · **IEEE Transactions on AI**  
**arXiv:** [2606.10706](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10706) | cs.LG

A constraint-centric survey organizing progress around three coupled bottlenecks in LLM training and fine-tuning: data efficiency (selection/pruning strategies), memory efficiency (jointly reducing weight storage, optimizer states, and activation memory), and compute budget awareness (when and where to spend FLOPs). A key finding is that GPU memory, not raw compute, is often the dominant bottleneck in fine-tuning, and that optimal data subsets depend on the task objective and resource budget.

### 5. Reinforcement Learning for Neural Model Editing
**Submitted:** June 11, 2026  
**arXiv:** [2606.13461](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13461) | cs.LG

Frames neural model editing (including fine-tuning-style modifications) as an RL problem where agents learn to modify pretrained networks via reward feedback. Introduces two environments — MaskWorld (multiplicative weight scaling) and ShiftWorld (additive updates) — and evaluates on bias mitigation in text classification and machine unlearning in image classification. Learned policies reduce forget-set accuracy to ~0% while preserving >90% retain-set accuracy, and improve bias-related performance by >5% without manual algorithm engineering.
