# Top 5 LLM Fine-Tuning Papers on arXiv This Week

---

- **NebulaExp-8B: An Empirical Post-Training Pipeline via Full-Scale Ablation Research**
  Published: 2026-06-25
  Abstract: A fully transparent, ablation-driven post-training pipeline on Qwen3-8B covering 3-stage optimized SFT, GRPO reinforcement learning, and verifier-free on-policy distillation (OPD/MOPD), achieving +5.98 points on instruct benchmarks and +4.43 average overall gain with just 4K samples.
  arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26671

- **RAFT: Data Refinement and Adaptive Distillation for Domain Fine-Tuning with Alleviated Forgetting**
  Published: 2026-05-29
  Abstract: Proposes a two-stage framework combining self-conditioned data rewriting and answer-conditioned on-policy distillation to address catastrophic forgetting in domain-specific SFT, improving domain accuracy by 23.2% while recovering 18.2% of SFT-induced general capability degradation.
  arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00147

- **Compatibility-Aware Dynamic Fine-Tuning for Large Language Models (ACL 2026)**
  Published: 2026-04-22
  Abstract: Introduces CADFT, a principled extension of Dynamic Fine-Tuning that controls sample-level optimization variance via policy-dependent compatibility signals, improving SFT stability, generalization, and cold-start RL initialization without explicit reward models.
  arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11206

- **Learning Rate Matters: Vanilla LoRA May Suffice for LLM Fine-tuning**
  Published: 2026-02-04 (revised 2026-05-19)
  Abstract: Systematically re-evaluates nine LoRA variants against vanilla LoRA across reasoning, coding, and instruction tasks — finding that once learning rates are properly tuned, all methods achieve similar peak performance (within 1–2%), suggesting vanilla LoRA remains a competitive baseline.
  arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04998

- **Attention-guided Fine-tuning of Multimodal Large Language Models Improves Chain-of-Thought Reasoning**
  Published: 2026-06-01
  Abstract: Identifies two failure modes in MLLM CoT reasoning (premature answer commitment and limited visual-token access) and proposes Attentive-CoT (Att-CoT), an attention-guided fine-tuning objective that plugs into any CoT-SFT training run and consistently improves visual reasoning across six MLLMs.
  arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01558
